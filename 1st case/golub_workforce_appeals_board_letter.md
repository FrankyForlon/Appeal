2. **Immediate Protest.** That evening I e‑mailed Associate Directors Wyatt Bentley and Brenda Byrnes demanding reinstatement and protesting the false allegation. Granite did not respond for eight days.

4. **May 15 Phone Call & E‑mail Shutdown.** During a scheduled call intended to discuss AI‑literacy curricula, Mr. Bentley refused to address the May 5 incident. Within hours Granite permanently closed my district e‑mail account, cutting off all communication.

### III. Grounds for Reversal
1. **Employer‑Initiated Separation Under Governing Rule.** Utah Admin. Code **R994‑405‑201** states: “*A separation is a discharge if the employer was the moving party in determining the date the employment ended.*” By locking me out of Frontline and deactivating every credential, Granite made further work impossible—an involuntary discharge, not a quit. See also *Clearfield City v. Dep’t of Emp’t Sec.*, 663 P.2d 440, 445 (Utah 1983) (benefits may be denied only where employee’s fault justifies termination). 

2. **No Showing of Culpability, Knowledge, or Control.** The Department’s three‑factor test for “just cause” (culpability, knowledge, control), upheld in *Kehl v. Bd. of Review of the Indus. Comm’n*, 700 P.2d 1129, 1135 (Utah 1985), requires evidence that the claimant knowingly violated a reasonable rule. Granite offered none. Its own witness could not recall my alleged resignation, and the ALJ acknowledged that gap. 

3. **Burden Misallocated.** R994‑405‑105 places the initial burden on the employer to prove a voluntary quit. By forcing me to *disprove* a quit, the ALJ inverted the rule, warranting reversal.

4. **Reliance on Undisclosed Evidence.** The decision quotes internal Granite e‑mails that were never provided after my accounts were shut down. R994‑508‑118 forbids reliance on undisclosed exhibits absent a written good‑cause finding, which is absent.

5. **Findings Contrary to the Record.** The ALJ simultaneously found that Granite “had not decided to discharge” me yet admitted my accounts were disabled. Those findings cannot coexist. Moreover, the supposed statement “I don’t plan to work for the District” appears nowhere; the only direct evidence is Mr. Bentley’s memory lapse at 46:26 of the recording.

6. **Equity‑and‑Good‑Conscience Ignored.** Even if the Board classifies the separation as a quit, R994‑405‑103 mandates an equity‑and‑good‑conscience analysis. I filed timely claims, searched diligently for work, and remained attached to the labour market—the precise behaviour the rule protects.

7. **Retaliatory Hostile Environment.** The May 5 K‑9 deployment, followed by instant deactivation of my credentials, created an intolerable work environment satisfying any constructive‑discharge standard.
